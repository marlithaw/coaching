Phase 1: Reflection (Hearing the Beat)

The R³ Model begins exactly where you are.

Reflection is the practice of seeing clearly what's happening beneath the automatic response. Like a producer isolating an audio track, it’s about separating the raw truth from the emotional noise — while it's happening. This is where your work begins.

Phase 2: Resistance (Hitting Pause)

You've done the listening. Now comes the pause.

Resistance is where automaticity—the conditioned reflex that skips like a scratched record—gets interrupted. By hitting pause on the track, conscious choice becomes possible. The R³ Model gives you a structure for exactly this space.

Phase 3: Revelation (Flipping the Sample)

You've found the sample. Now make it your default.

Purposeful Practicing — applying insight consistently — is how neuroplasticity works. You have to play the new beat repeatedly until the nervous system updates its default rhythm. That's what Phase 3 builds toward.

Phase 1: Reflection

RReflectWhat▾
Reflection is not about reliving or judging the moment. It is about looking at it clearly enough to understand what it is showing you. You can't remix a song if you don't listen to the original sample first.
AAware & AttentiveHow▾
Aware and Attentive are the two points of analysis.

Aware — What emotional or physical load did you bring into this moment? You are naming the background noise that was already playing.

Attentive — What is your body doing right now? Jaw tight. Shoulders elevated. The body registers the threat before the mind names it. This is your rhythm section telling you something is off.
PPurposeful PonderingWhy▾
Purposeful Pondering asks: what is this moment actually showing me, and what does that make possible? This is not spiraling into always and never thinking. It is active inquiry—transforming observation into direction so you can write a better verse.

Phase 2: Resistance

RResistWhat▾
The intentional interruption of the conditioned response (inhibitory control)—like catching the needle before it drops on a bad track. Resistance is the practice of hitting pause long enough to choose whether to press forward or change direction based on consciousness, not habit.
AActHow▾
Remaining present creates conditions for intentional action. Act moves through three layers:

Pattern Recognition — Am I playing the default track or making a choice?

Alignment Check — Does this align with my values and what I am trying to preserve?

Consequence Awareness — What does my action produce? What is the cost of this verse?
PPurposeful PressingWhy▾
When direction is informed by concrete actions rather than habit, choice becomes the new evidence the nervous system needs. You practice it until the intentional response replaces the scratched record, leading to lasting growth.

Phase 3: Revelation

RRevealWhat▾
Revelation is the AH-HA moment: the point at which objective awareness and conscious choice combine to generate truth about yourself. Revelation is not another round of inward examination—it is flipping the sample to find the gold.
AAcceptHow▾
The practice of receiving the truth and integrating it.

Receive the insight — Name the pattern recognized, not just the feeling.

Integrate without judgment — Holding the truth without shame or deflection. The AH-HA is not the end of the process. It is the moment the process becomes productive.
PPurposeful PracticingWhy▾
The truth compounds through consistent application. The nervous system does not update on insight alone (neuroplasticity requires repetition). Purposeful Practicing is the commitment to playing the new beat continuously, generating new evidence with each cycle until you've permanently changed your rhythm.
